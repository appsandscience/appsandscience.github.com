Getting Started on App Development With a Little Help From Canappi

Jan 23rd, 2012

Developing simple data-driven apps for the iPhone is not simple. Objective-C, the native language of the iPhone, is not easy, and accomplishing simple tasks with can be quite complicated.

I realized that when I tried to create a simple menu-driven application that catalogs all the Chemistry apps on the Apple store.

Just a bit of background: I have chosen to classify existing Chemistry apps in the following five categories:

  1. Exam preparation

  2. Chemistry-related calculators

  3. Books

  4. Reference works

  5. Games

Now imagine an iPhone application that has a tab bar at the bottom with two tabs: One, called “Featured” and another called “Categories.” When you tap on one of the categories you can see a list of related apps, “App 1,” “App 2,” etc. When you tap on the “Featured” tab you can see a list of featured apps, “Featured App 1,” “Featured App 2,” etc.
